Attractions

Manitoba Legislative Building

The Manitoba Legislative Building is the seat of the provincial government. It’s known for its impressive architecture and the Golden Boy statue on top.

Image via Wikimedia Commons

Canadian Museum for Human Rights

This museum is dedicated to the history and importance of human rights. Its modern design is one of Winnipeg’s most recognizable landmarks.

Image via Wikimedia Commons

The Forks

The Forks is a popular gathering place where visitors can explore shops, restaurants, and walking paths near the river.

Image via flickr

Key points in Winnipeg history

Year Event Significance
1738 Fort Rouge established First European presence in Winnipeg area
1873 Winnipeg Incorporation Winnipeg officially inaugurated as a city
1919 Winnipeg General Strike Largest strike in Canadian history
1999 Opening of Human Rights Museum First national museum outside of Ottawa opens
